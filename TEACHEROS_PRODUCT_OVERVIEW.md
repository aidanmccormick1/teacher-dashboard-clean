# TeacherOS Product Overview

## What TeacherOS Is
TeacherOS is a planning and classroom workflow app built for teachers who teach multiple class periods, manage shared curriculum, and need a simple way to know what to teach next.

It connects the pieces teachers normally manage separately:

- courses,
- class periods,
- weekly schedule,
- year plan,
- lesson segments,
- section progress,
- carry-over notes,
- daily classroom flow.

The goal is simple:

Teachers should be able to open the app and immediately understand today.

## The Problem
Most teacher planning tools are either too simple or too complicated.

Simple tools usually track tasks, notes, or calendars, but they do not understand how teaching actually works across multiple class periods.

Complex tools often feel like school administration software. They can be powerful, but they are slow, rigid, and not designed around a teacher moving through a real teaching day.

Teachers often have to answer questions like:

- What class do I have next?
- Where did Period 2 stop yesterday?
- Is Period 4 ahead or behind Period 1?
- What lesson should I teach today?
- Did I already finish that segment with this section?
- What needs to carry over into tomorrow?
- How does a missed day affect the rest of the unit?

TeacherOS is designed around those daily questions.

## The Core Idea
A course is the shared plan.

A period is a real group of students attached to that course.

Each period can move through the same course at a different pace.

Example:

- Course: US History
- Periods: Period 1, Period 3, Period 5
- All three use the same US History year plan.
- Period 1 might be on Lesson 8.
- Period 3 might be on Lesson 7.
- Period 5 might be on Lesson 9.

TeacherOS makes those differences visible and useful.

## What The App Helps Teachers Do

### 1. Set Up Courses
Teachers create the courses they teach, such as:

- US History
- Algebra I
- English 9
- Biology
- Advisory

Each course becomes the home for its year plan, units, lessons, and lesson segments.

### 2. Add Class Periods
Teachers add the real periods or sections they teach.

Each period attaches to a course.

That means multiple periods can share the same course plan while still tracking progress separately.

### 3. Build A Weekly Schedule
Teachers define when each period meets.

The schedule helps the app understand:

- what is happening today,
- what class is next,
- which period is currently active,
- which sections may be missing meeting times or rooms.

### 4. Build A Year Plan
Teachers can plan the course across the school year.

The year plan can include:

- units,
- lessons,
- lesson segments,
- estimated timing,
- section progress,
- pacing view.

This makes curriculum feel like something teachers can actually use during the day, not a document that sits somewhere else.

### 5. Track Section Progress
Each class period tracks progress independently.

TeacherOS can show:

- where each section is,
- which section is ahead,
- which section is behind,
- what segment was last completed,
- what needs to be taught next.

### 6. Resume Classroom Flow
Classroom Mode is designed to help a teacher pick up from the right place.

Instead of guessing what happened last class, the teacher can see:

- current lesson,
- completed segments,
- stopped-at point,
- carry-over note,
- next teaching move.

## The Daily Dashboard
The dashboard is the teacher’s daily command center.

It answers:

- What is happening today?
- Am I ready?
- What class is active now?
- What class is next?
- What still needs setup?
- What schedule or curriculum gaps need attention?

The dashboard is not meant to be a dense analytics page.

It should feel calm, quick, and useful.

## Why This Is Different
TeacherOS is not just a planner.

It understands the relationship between:

- the course plan,
- the class periods,
- the schedule,
- lesson progress,
- daily teaching decisions.

That connection is the product.

The strongest feature is not one individual screen. It is the way the app can help a teacher move from planning to teaching without losing context.

## Who It Is For
TeacherOS is especially useful for:

- middle school teachers,
- high school teachers,
- teachers with multiple sections of the same course,
- teachers who need to track where each class period stopped,
- teachers building curriculum over time,
- teachers who want a simpler alternative to scattered docs, calendars, and notes.

It is designed for teachers who may not be technical, but who still need advanced planning power when they are ready for it.

## Product Promise
TeacherOS should help teachers feel:

- less scattered,
- more prepared,
- more aware of where each class is,
- less dependent on memory,
- more confident walking into the next period.

The app should not make teachers feel like they are doing extra administrative work.

It should feel like the app is remembering the school year with them.

## Practical AI, Not AI For Show
AI should not be the center of TeacherOS.

AI should help with practical teacher tasks only, such as:

- reading a schedule from pasted text or an uploaded file,
- breaking lessons into segments,
- drafting a starter year plan,
- suggesting pacing after a missed day,
- cleaning up notes,
- suggesting a catch-up plan for a behind section.

All AI output should be reviewable before it changes real data.

The teacher stays in control.

## Why Teachers Might Want To Test It
TeacherOS is worth testing because it is aimed at a real pain point:

Teachers often teach the same course more than once a day, but each period moves differently.

Most tools do not handle that elegantly.

TeacherOS is trying to make that difference simple, visible, and useful.

Teachers should test it if they want to see whether one app can help them manage:

- what they teach,
- when they teach it,
- where each period is,
- what comes next.

## What A Good First Test Looks Like
A teacher can test TeacherOS with a small setup:

1. Create one course.
2. Add two or three periods for that course.
3. Add meeting days and times.
4. Add one unit.
5. Add two lessons.
6. Add a few lesson segments.
7. Use Classroom Mode to mark where each period stops.

That is enough to test the main product idea.

The app does not need a full year of data before it becomes useful.

## Current Product Direction
The current direction is:

- simpler navigation,
- calmer dashboard,
- stronger Management hub,
- clearer course-to-period model,
- better Year Plan and pacing views,
- more useful Classroom Mode,
- practical import tools,
- production-ready hosting and reliability.

The product should keep becoming more powerful without feeling more complicated.
